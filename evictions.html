The eviction notices, and the Council vote that backed them

On 28 May 2026 the Council voted to support a policy that caps how long anyone may stay at the Flax Mill Caravan Park. Notices were then served on long-term residents requiring them to vacate.

The Council vote — 28 May 2026

At the Ordinary Council Meeting of 28 May 2026, Item 10.4.2 introduced a Short Term Stay Policy for the Flax Mill Caravan Park. Council resolved to support its implementation (Decision CM 26/05/117).

The policy document tabled with that item was CMCA's own Guest Stay Policy, reference CMCA-GSP-FMB-001.

The eviction notices

Following the vote, residents received a "Notice of Stay Limit Enforcement & Required Vacation of Site" issued under the CMCA policy (CMCA-GSP-FMB-001), requiring them to leave by 19 June 2026.

Those affected include people supporting the shearing and health industries, infrastructure and road workers, and residents who have no alternative housing in a town with no available rentals.

Why it matters

The Shire's own monthly reports record, month after month, that demand for longer-term accommodation came from essential workers and from people with nowhere else to live. The caretaker's reports recommended reviewing the maximum stay period — not eviction. The Monthly Data section sets out that record in full, and The Law section sets out the legal problems with how the notices were issued.
