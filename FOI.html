Public Record — Freedom of Information

Freedom of Information

On 29 June 2026 a Freedom of Information request was lodged with the Shire of Boyup Brook seeking the documents and authority behind the Guest Stay Policy, the vacation notices, and the way the Flax Mill Caravan Park is being run. The request sets out the law it relies on, and is accompanied by a schedule listing each Act, regulation, planning provision and agreement clause referred to.

Both documents are published in full below. The legislation itself — the Caravan Parks and Camping Grounds Act 1995, the Residential Parks (Long-stay Tenants) Act 2006, their Regulations, and the related planning and agreement provisions — is summarised in the Schedule of References.

The Freedom of Information request — 29 June 2026

The request asks the Shire, as the deemed licence holder under section 15 of the Caravan Parks and Camping Grounds Act 1995, for the source material behind the monthly caretaker reports, the development of the Guest Stay Policy, the implementation of the vacation notices, the legal authority claimed for the "Essential Worker" exemption, and the Shire's authority to operate the park as "short stay only".

Schedule of References — the legislation relied on

This schedule lists the legislation, regulations, local planning scheme provisions, management agreement clauses and Shire strategic documents referred to in the request — including the definition of a "long stay site" under regulation 3 of the Caravan Parks and Camping Grounds Regulations 1997, and section 5 of the Residential Parks (Long-stay Tenants) Act 2006.
