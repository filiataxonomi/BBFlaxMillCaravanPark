Public Record — Caravan Park Booking Data

Monthly caravan park data

Every month the caravan park's bookings and income are reported to Council. From August 2025 onward, the caretaker's report recorded — in nearly every month — demand for longer-term accommodation from essential workers and from people with nowhere else to live.

Every figure below is taken directly from Shire of Boyup Brook Council minutes and agendas. The income is shared 73% to the Shire, 27% to CMCA.

August 2025

Source: Minutes, OCM 25 September 2025, Item 9.4.6. View ↗

213 nights · $5,236 gross · Shire share $3,822 · 30% CMCA members. A "very wet and cold August" noted.

The first month the caretaker recorded housing-shortage demand:

The CMCA Caretaker reports that there are several patrons supporting the shearing industry and health industry who are seeking longer term accommodation due to the lack of available housing in Boyup Brook. There have also been several families staying overnight who are looking at properties to purchase to relocate to Boyup Brook.

January 2026

Source: Minutes, OCM 26 February 2026, Item 10.4.3.

544 nights · $13,340 gross · Shire share $9,738 · 45% CMCA members. Revenue up 18%, nights up 39% on January 2025.

This report marks an escalation. The caretaker recorded:

Demand for longer term accommodation continues to grow, with a significant increase in requests for permanent residency being received. … There has been a notable increase in requests from outlying regions, including metropolitan areas, driven by ongoing housing shortages and affordability.
It is recommended that the Shire and CMCA re-assess the maximum stay period … to establish guidelines that are compliant, balanced, and that continue to support those requiring shorter term accommodation, without adversely impacting tourism.

The recommendation was to reassess the maximum stay — not to evict.

February 2026

Source: Minutes, OCM 26 March 2026, Item 10.4.1.

782 nights · $20,370 gross · Shire share $14,870 · 31% CMCA members. 218 nights during the Country Music Festival. Extrapolated local spend: $50,544.

There has also been an increase in patrons seeking long term accommodation of two to three months associated with infrastructure and project works, e.g. road works, Water Corporation projects, and a recent enquiry from a mineral resource exploration company …
The current approach is to limit stays to two months, with any extension … considered on a case-by-case basis … with a priority for critical workers and patrons with employment or project works within the Shire.

March 2026

Source: Minutes, OCM 30 April 2026, Item 10.4.1.

488 nights · $13,617 gross · Shire share $9,940. Revenue up 41%, nights up 41% on March 2025. Waterwise plumbing upgrades completed ($25,060, funded by Water Corporation).

Long term residency requests are primarily associated with essential workers and individuals engaged in local industries such as shearing, infrastructure projects, road works, and health services. In some instances, longer stays are also being sought due to the limited availability of affordable housing within the region.

April 2026

Source: Minutes, OCM 28 May 2026 (unconfirmed), Item 10.4.1. View ↗

589 nights · $15,484 gross · Shire share $11,344. Revenue up 36.5%, nights up 23% on April 2025. "Occupancy largely driven by longer stay visitors."

The last monthly report before the Short Term Stay Policy was introduced at the very same meeting (Item 10.4.2):

While the increase in longer term occupancy assists in meeting workforce accommodation needs, it also presents a risk that the Caravan Park may be perceived as facilitating longer term or semi-permanent residency. This is being actively monitored.

May 2026 — the first report after the notices

Source: Agenda, OCM 25 June 2026, Item 10.4.1, pages 24–27 (officer's monthly report; confirmed minutes not yet published).

496 nights · $14,552 gross · Shire share $10,623. Shire's revenue up 50% on May 2025; nights up 47% (up 158 nights); CMCA member stays up 55%. Occupancy down on April. Member-to-non-member ratio 3:2 (60% : 40%).

Unlike every report from August 2025 to April 2026, this report — the first after the Short Term Stay Policy and the vacation notices were introduced — makes no reference to longer-term stays, essential workers or the housing shortage. It is framed solely around short-term tourism and visitor demand.
